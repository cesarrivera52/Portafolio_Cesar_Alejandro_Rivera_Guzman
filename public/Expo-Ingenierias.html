Operational Performance Analytics Dashboard

OEE | KPI's Development | Data Visualization

April - July 2024

Situation

In this project, we collaborated with GESSA Salaverry, a company dedicated to manufacturing plastic film products. The company requested operational consultancy within the process area, aiming to reinforce its continuous improvement philosophy and identify opportunities for performance optimization.

To better understand the production environment, we conducted multiple plant visits, analyzed available operational data, and interviewed operators and managers to gather insights regarding workflow efficiency, material usage, and performance tracking.

Through on-site observation and process analysis, we identified inefficiencies related to raw material utilization and workflow bottlenecks that were limiting production performance.

The Value Stream Mapping (VSM) analysis enabled us to visualize the end-to-end process and pinpoint the primary bottleneck affecting operational flow. Additionally, we detected gaps in data collection practices, as certain machines were not systematically recording operational metrics. This limited the accuracy of Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE) calculations and reduced visibility into real production performance.

Task

Following the identification of bottlenecks and data gaps, we focused on improving the operational data collection system. Machine performance metrics were being manually recorded in handwritten logs, leading to data inconsistencies and limited reliability.

This lack of structured data directly affected the accuracy of OEE calculations and restricted management’s ability to make data-driven production decisions.

Our goal was to automate the operational data registration process and develop a structured visualization tool to monitor machine efficiency and performance trends.

Action

After digitizing the handwritten operational records, we structured and transformed the dataset to enable meaningful analysis. This involved data cleaning, standardization, and categorization to ensure consistency and reliability.

The first phase of our solution focused on automating the data collection process to reduce manual errors and improve traceability. The second phase added analytical value through the development of an interactive dashboard capable of visualizing both macro-level KPIs and micro-level machine performance metrics.

The dashboard allowed users to monitor production efficiency, identify performance deviations, and analyze operational trends in a clear and structured format.

Results

The image above presents the interactive dashboard we developed. It enables real-time visualization of the Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE) KPI, providing both macro-level operational insights and detailed micro-level machine analysis.

The platform allows users to dynamically filter performance data by machine, production date, and additional operational parameters, facilitating structured analysis and data-driven decision-making.

The implementation improved operational visibility and enabled more accurate OEE analysis through structured data collection.

Due to the impact and quality of the project, we were invited to present our solution at the Engineering Fair at Tecnológico de Monterrey, Campus Puebla. Additionally, the company offered us the opportunity to continue developing the project during the summer period.
