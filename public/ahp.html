AHP Decision-Making Model

Multi-Decision Criteria Maker / Lean / Warehouse Optimization

April 2024 - June 2024

Situation

During this project the enterprise had many pieces and components in their warehouse, the storage of this items represented a big amount of money spent and a space storage.

The challenge we faced as a group was to develop a system capable to help the managers to take decisions based on their needs.

Task

We applied some topics seen in class that could help us to develop a system. In this case we used the AHP method.

To accomplish this task we used the AHP (Analytic Hierarchy Process) methodology, this method is a structured technique for organizing and analyzing complex decisions, based on mathematics and psychology. It was developed by Thomas L. Saaty in the 1970s and has been extensively studied and refined since then.

To give a better example of what we did it will be explained with an example.

Example: Choosing the Best Smartphone

To illustrate how AHP works, let's consider a simple example of choosing the best smartphone among three alternatives: Phone A, Phone B, and Phone C. The decision is based on three criteria: Price, Battery Life, and Camera Quality.

Step 1: Hierarchy

Goal: Choose the best smartphone

  • Criteria
    • Price
    • Battery Life
    • Camera Quality
  • Alternatives
    • Phone A
    • Phone B
    • Phone C

Step 2: Criteria Pairwise Comparison

The relative importance of each criterion is compared using a scale from 1 (equal importance) to 9 (extremely more important):

Criteria Price Battery Camera
Price 1 3 5
Battery 1/3 1 2
Camera 1/5 1/2 1

Step 3: Criteria Weights

  • Price: 0.648
  • Battery Life: 0.230
  • Camera Quality: 0.122

Step 4: Alternatives Comparison

Pairwise comparison for each criterion:

Price
  • Phone A: 0.57
  • Phone B: 0.30
  • Phone C: 0.13
Battery Life
  • Phone A: 0.20
  • Phone B: 0.50
  • Phone C: 0.30
Camera Quality
  • Phone A: 0.25
  • Phone B: 0.35
  • Phone C: 0.40

Step 5: Overall Scores

Phone Score
Phone A 0.455
Phone B 0.362
Phone C 0.183

Decision

Phone A has the highest overall score and is therefore the best choice according to the criteria and preferences defined.

Action

The example above describes what we tried to do for the managers to determine what items should leave the system.
