MR35 Device Improvement | AHP Based-Priorization Model

February 2023 - June 2023 | April 2024 - June 2024

Context

During my time as an Industrial and Systems Engineering student, I had the opportunity to collaborate with our academic training partner, Audi México.

Audi México, located in San José Chiapa, Puebla, is dedicated to the production of high-end vehicles such as the Audi Q5. The manufacturing process begins with body preparation and premium painting, followed by the assembly of mechanical, electronic, and safety components. Automated systems are integrated with specialized human intervention to ensure precision, efficiency, and compliance with strict quality standards.

Within this manufacturing environment, we developed two independent projects addressing different operational challenges.

Project 1 – MR35 Ergonomic Improvement

The first initiative focused on modifying the MR35 transport device to increase load capacity while reducing ergonomic risk for operators responsible for moving automotive components from the warehouse to various production areas.

The redesign aimed to improve operational efficiency, enhance safety conditions, and minimize physical strain during material handling activities.

Project 2 – AHP Decision-Making Model

The second project involved developing an Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP) decision model to support warehouse dispatch prioritization.

The automated matrix enabled structured multi-criteria evaluation of components, assisting management in determining which parts should be released from the warehouse system based on strategic and operational factors.
