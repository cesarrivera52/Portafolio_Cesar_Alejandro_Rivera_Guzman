Operational Efficiency & Production Analysis

Process Evaluation| Production Analysis

September - November 2023

Situation

For this project, we collaborated with Truchas San José, a company dedicated to the farming and commercialization of trout, with over 30 years of operational experience.

Despite its long-standing presence in the market, the company sought to evaluate its strategic positioning and internal business processes to identify improvement opportunities.

Our objective was to analyze operational workflows, commercial strategies, and performance indicators in order to detect inefficiencies and propose structured improvements to enhance competitiveness and long-term sustainability.

Task

To identify improvement opportunities, we conducted a primary data collection process through structured interviews with key stakeholders, including owners and operational staff.

The objective of the interviews was to understand the company’s operational model, commercial strategy, resource management, and growth constraints. Open-ended questions were used to encourage detailed responses and uncover qualitative insights regarding internal processes and strategic positioning.

Through our analysis, we identified that Truchas San José follows a differentiation strategy supported by the following competitive advantages:

  • Fresh, farm-to-table trout products served directly in their restaurant
  • Operational self-sufficiency through solar panel energy generation
  • Dedicated delivery truck for product distribution
  • Lean operational structure with three operators managing daily activities
  • Unique rural dining concept offering an authentic countryside experience
  • Low spoilage rate (< 8%), supported by flexible growth cycles in trout fattening

The production model minimizes waste by allowing trout that experience slower growth to remain in the fattening process until reaching the desired commercial size. Spoilage occurs only in cases of mortality during production.

Action

After identifying the performance gap, we determined that reducing trout mortality was the primary operational priority. The absence of standardized data collection prevented the company from analyzing mortality patterns and identifying root causes.

As an initial solution, we designed and implemented a structured Excel-based data recording system. The tool included standardized fields to track pond identification, date, mortality count, environmental observations, and operational variables.

This digital format replaced informal record-keeping practices and enabled systematic monitoring of mortality trends.

Results

The final proposal presentation is available in the PDF below. It summarizes the analysis performed, key insights identified, and the strategic value of implementing systematic data collection practices.

The presentation also includes data visualizations that highlight mortality patterns and identify the ponds with the highest death rates, enabling targeted operational interventions.
